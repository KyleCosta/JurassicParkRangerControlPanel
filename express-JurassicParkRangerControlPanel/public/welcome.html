WELCOME TO JURASSIC PARK SAN DIEGO!

We're thrilled to have you on board as a valued member of our team at Jurassic Park. As pioneers in the field of dinosaur-themed attractions, we have learned from the experiences of the past and are committed to creating a safe and enjoyable environment for both our employees and visitors.

CODE OF CONDUCT

In the wake of the past Jurassic Park incidents, our dedication to safety is more critical than ever, considering the heightened intelligence displayed by our Velociraptors and the inherent danger posed by all dinosaurs under our care. Employees must be acutely aware of the unique challenges presented by each species, particularly the Velociraptors, who possess a remarkable ability to discern weaknesses in enclosures.

Vigilance is paramount. Personnel should not only conduct thorough checks of enclosures but also exercise extreme caution when maintaining enclosures and feeding dinosaurs. Recognizing the inherent dangers associated with these creatures, especially the Velociraptors, is imperative.

Furthermore, employees should engage in continuous training to stay ahead of the evolving intelligence of our prehistoric inhabitants. This proactive approach, coupled with strict adherence to safety protocols, ensures the well-being of both our team and the park's visitors. By upholding these standards, we create an environment where the marvels of Jurassic Park San Diego can be appreciated with confidence and without compromising the safety of anyone within our dynamic ecosystem.

DINOSAUR ESCAPE EMERGENCY PROCEDURE

  • Guest Evacuation:
    Remain calm and assist guests to the nearest evacuation point.
    Use established evacuation routes and guide guests away from the
    affected area.
  • Radio Communication:
    Contact emergency services and the park's command center.
    Coordinate with local authorities for evacuation routes and updates.
  • Confronted by a Dinosaur:
    Herbivores: Encourage calm behavior, avoid sudden movements,
    and slowly back away.
    Carnivores: Avoid direct eye contact, move slowly without turning your back,
    and retreat to a safe location.
  • Guest Attack:
    If a guest is attacked by a dinosaur, immediately contact on-site
    medical personnel.
    Secure the area and provide first aid until medical help arrives.
